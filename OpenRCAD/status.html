Blending & offset — what works today

Phase 5 geometry is in place, and the blend builders work end-to-end on the two primitive families the detectors recognize. Each returns a watertight solid (sewn) or a typed error.

OperationBoxCylinderConstruction
fillet yes yes Box: cylindrical edge faces + spherical octant corners. Cylinder: torus rolling-ball faces on both rims.
chamfer yes yes Box: ruled bevel faces + triangular corners. Cylinder: 45° conical frustum on both rims.
shell_solid yes yes Offset-surface inner cavity + planar rim faces around the removed open faces.

Scope limit, stated plainly. The whole-solid blend builders (fillet/chamfer/shell_solid) detect a single box or cylinder primitive — at any position/orientation (the frame is recovered from geometry, verified watertight on rotated inputs) — and construct the result directly; for any other whole solid they return BlendError::UnsupportedShape (they never silently hand back the input). The per-edge rolling-ball fillet (fillet_edges) does handle arbitrary planar/analytic edges, including boolean results, and rejects an over-large radius instead of emitting a degenerate solid. N-valent Gregory corner blends, face overflow, and concave-offset self-intersection resolution are not yet implemented.

Booleans — watertight + health-validated

Booleans run over the BVH-accelerated intersection engine and finish watertight and healthy (is_healthy — contiguous loops, no degenerate edges, manifold) across the everyday cases, tracked by crates/openrcad-algo/tests/robustness.rs and the repro_* suites. Coplanar adjacent faces are merged back to clean topology (a two-box union is a 6-face box). No boolean goal-tests remain #[ignore]d.

The split pass imprints partial and coplanar intersection curves (a coplanar circular cap is bored as a hole, closing cylinder bosses), and sew re-threads every loop into a contiguous co-edge chain so results pass validate()/is_healthy(), not just is_watertight().

New since the last cut

  • Boolean robustness & clean topology — loop re-threading in sew makes results pass is_healthy() (so boolean_checked accepts ordinary joins on thin/off-axis/coplanar geometry); coplanar + collinear merge collapses a two-box union to a clean 6-face box; a coplanar circular-hole imprint closes cylinder boss unions. The per-edge fillet now works on boolean results and errors on an over-large radius.
  • ~10× faster booleans — a closed-form curve_curve fast path for line/circle pairs (lines have infinite bounds, so the old subdivision bisected a clamped ±100 domain) dropped box∩box from ~13.8 ms to ~1.3 ms; the classification scan is now BVH-pruned.
  • Watertight / manifold validation — Solid::is_watertight, manifold_report, is_manifold, and health_report, with a regression suite that asserts closure on every verified-good boolean and blend (and pins the failure frontier).
  • Interactive viewer (openrcad-render) — orbit / pan / zoom, 4× MSAA, a topological-edge wireframe overlay, and click-to-select with a shader face highlight.
  • Orientation-general blends — fillet / chamfer / shell verified watertight on rotated boxes and cylinders, not just axis-aligned.
  • Parametric layer — openrcad-sketch (2D profiles) and openrcad-document (feature recompute + the .zcad document format).
  • ToroidalSurface — a first-class analytic surface wired through the geometry enum, the BVH, intersection uv_of, tessellation, and STEP read/write (TOROIDAL_SURFACE).
  • Clippy-clean workspace — every lint addressed; numeric kernels keep index loops by explicit, justified allow.
